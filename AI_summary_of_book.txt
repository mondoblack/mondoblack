Mondoblack: A Story Summary

This story, "Mondoblack," explores themes of technological displacement, the
search for meaning in a post-work society, and the human connection in a world
increasingly dominated by artificial intelligence.

Plot Summary:

  In a future where AIs have taken over most jobs, Paul, a former IT professional,
  navigates a world of universal basic income and co-operative economies. He
  attends an art show with his wife Karen, her sister Susan, and brother-in-law
  David. The central piece, "Mondoblack," a door leading into seemingly infinite
  blackness, triggers a series of introspective experiences for Paul. The story
  follows Paul's journey as he grapples with feelings of obsolescence and searches
  for purpose. He has a vivid dream related to the Mondoblack, and later nearly
  drowns in a pool, experiencing a profound sense of connection to the void.
  Ultimately, he realizes the importance of human connection and decides to start
  a family with Karen, finding meaning in their relationship and future. The story
  also includes a subplot about teenagers stealing the Mondoblack artwork, driven
  by a desire for excitement and a connection to something beyond the mundane.

Key Themes:

   Technological Displacement: The story explores the impact of AI on employment
   and the resulting search for meaning in a post-work society.  The Search for
   Meaning: Characters grapple with feelings of obsolescence and seek purpose
   through co-operative work, art, and personal relationships.  Human

   increasingly dominated by technology is emphasized.  The Nature of Reality:

   the potential for both fear and transformation when confronting the void.

Character and Theme Analysis:

  Paul's journey is central to the story's exploration of these themes. His
  initial feelings of displacement and depression are contrasted with Karen's
  grounded perspective and her dedication to helping others. The Mondoblack
  artwork acts as a mirror, reflecting Paul's inner turmoil and prompting him to
  confront his fears and insecurities. His near-drowning experience and subsequent
  realization about the importance of his relationship with Karen represent a
  turning point, as he finds a renewed sense of purpose in building a future with
  her. The subplot involving the teenagers stealing the Mondoblack highlights a
